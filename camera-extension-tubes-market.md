<p><p>Camera extension tubes are used to increase the distance between the camera lens and the image sensor, allowing for closer focusing on subjects. The market is divided into two primary types: automatic and manual extension tubes. Automatic extension tubes feature electronic contacts that communicate with the camera, maintaining autofocus and aperture settings, making them user-friendly. In contrast, manual extension tubes lack this functionality, requiring the photographer to adjust focus and aperture settings manually, offering more control but potentially less convenience.</p></p>
