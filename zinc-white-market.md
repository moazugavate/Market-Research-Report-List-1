<p><p>Zinc White, a crucial pigment in various industries, is produced through different methods. The Direct Process, or American Method, involves the direct oxidation of zinc. The Indirect Process, known as the French Method, utilizes zinc oxide heating in a furnace, resulting in finer particles. Meanwhile, the Wet Chemical Process produces Zinc White by treating zinc salts with alkalis, allowing for controlled particle size and purity. Each method caters to specific market needs, emphasizing quality, application, and production efficiency.</p></p>
