<p><p>The wine-making yeast market can be divided into two main types: yeast extract and autolyzed yeast. Yeast extract is derived from yeast cells and is used to enhance flavors in wine, providing essential nutrients and umami characteristics. Autolyzed yeast, on the other hand, is produced through the self-digestion of yeast cells, resulting in a rich source of amino acids and vitamins that contribute to mouthfeel and complexity in wine. Both types play crucial roles in fermentation and overall wine quality.</p></p>
