<p><p>Cervical interbody fusion cages are medical devices used in spinal surgeries, specifically for cervical spine stabilization. <p><p>Cervical interbody fusion cages are medical devices used to stabilize the spine during fusion procedures. The metal type, often made from titanium or stainless steel, offers strength and durability, facilitating long-term load-bearing support. In contrast, polymeric type cages, crafted from biocompatible materials like PEEK, provide flexibility and lower stiffness, promoting natural motion and potentially reducing stress on adjacent vertebrae. Both types aim to enhance spinal stability and promote successful fusion in cervical spine surgeries, addressing different patient needs and anatomical considerations.</p></p>
