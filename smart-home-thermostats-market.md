<p><p>Smart home thermostats can be categorized based on connectivity technologies like WiFi and ZigBee. WiFi thermostats connect directly to home networks, allowing remote control via smartphones or smart assistants, making them user-friendly and widely adopted. ZigBee technology, on the other hand, uses a mesh networking approach to enhance communication between devices, offering better energy efficiency and reliability in larger homes. Other technologies, such as Z-Wave and Bluetooth, also exist, providing varying levels of compatibility and features suited to different user needs.</p></p>
